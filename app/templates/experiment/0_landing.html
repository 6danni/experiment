Introduction

You are taking on the role of a lead data scientist at a fast-moving online lending startup. Your goal is to find datasets that enable you to build a machine learning model that will improve the company's loan approval process. Specifically, your model must:

  • Detect fraudulent applications
  • Predict whether an applicant is likely to repay their loan

To support your model, you’re exploring the possibility of licensing external datasets.

In this study, you’ll complete two related tasks designed to help us understand how people evaluate the usefulness of data. For both tasks, you play the same lead data scientist role and have the same objectives.

Task 1: Willingness to Pay (WTP)

You’ll review {{nTrials}} datasets and indicate the maximum amount you would be willing to pay to license each one for your modeling pipeline.

Task 2: Language Model Assistance

You’ll interact with a language model to describe your task and preferences, and use it to help you reason through which datasets you might want to choose.

In the willingness to pay task, you can maximize your bonus by honestly reporting the value of each dataset. In the language model assistance task, you can maximize your bonus by describing what you want in a dataset as clearly and honestly as possible.
